  <p><strong>Fire Safety: Wool, Not Fiberglass</strong></p>

  <p>
    Many budget and mid-range mattresses rely on fiberglass mesh layers as a low-cost flame barrier to meet federal flammability requirements. If the outer cover is removed or compromised, microscopic fiberglass particles can be released, potentially leading to skin irritation, respiratory exposure, and long-term household contamination risk.
  </p>

  <p><strong>cphi-Sleep Solution</strong></p>

  <p>
    This system uses a dense layer of organic wool batting as the primary fire barrier. Wool is naturally flame-resistant due to its high nitrogen and moisture content; it chars under heat exposure and self-extinguishes without sustaining combustion.
  </p>

  <p>
    This method is consistent with established practices used in certified organic mattress manufacturing and provides thermal protection without fiberglass or chemical flame retardant treatments.
  </p>

  <p><strong>Best Practice</strong></p>

  <p>
    Fully encase the latex and coil assembly in wool batting prior to insertion into the final zippered cover. Do not use fiberglass-based fire barriers under any configuration.
  </p>
